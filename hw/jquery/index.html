Italy in the Summer

Venice Florence Rome San Gregorio Nelle Alpi Tuscany

Italy in the summer can be a life changing experience. Especially for someone who has never been out of the country. The cities have breathtaking architecture and are almost spotless, and the countrysides will take your breath away.

If you're going to Italy, here are some tips:

  • Pack light, but warm
  • Take lots of pictures
  • Do research
  • Go with people you enjoy
  • Don't stress

Venice

Venice, the capital of northern Italy’s Veneto region, is built on more than 100 small islands in a lagoon in the Adriatic Sea. It has no roads, just canals – including the Grand Canal thoroughfare – lined with Renaissance and Gothic palaces.

Florence

Florence, capital of Italy’s Tuscany region, is home to many masterpieces of Renaissance art and architecture. One of its most iconic sights is the Duomo, a cathedral with a terracotta-tiled dome engineered by Brunelleschi and a bell tower by Giotto.

Rome

Rome is the capital city in Italy and the capital of the Lazio region. Rome is located in the largest populated commune in the country. It is home to numerous historical sights including the Colloseum, the Roman Forum and St. Peter's Basilica

San Gregorio Nelle Alpi

San Gregorio nelle Alpi is a comune in the Province of Belluno in the Italian region Veneto, located about 80 kilometres northwest of Venice and about 15 kilometres west of Belluno.

Tuscany

Tuscany is a region in central Italy. Its capital, Florence, is home to some of the world’s most recognizable Renaissance art and architecture, including Michelangelo’s "David" statue, Botticelli’s works in the Uffizi Gallery and the Duomo basilica.
